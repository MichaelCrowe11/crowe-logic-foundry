Why Foundry exists

Every other AI tool is a wrapper around one model. Crowe Logic runs two concurrently, reconciles them, and delivers one answer you can trust more than either alone.

Features

Built for operators who need more than chat.

◆

Dual-model concurrent

Run Claude Opus and Kimi K2.6 side-by-side on the same prompt. Watch both stream in real time. Compare reasoning paths before committing to an answer.

◆

Synthesis layer

A third model reads both responses and produces the final synthesis. Three modes: merge (blend strongest claims), judge (pick winner), diff (show gaps).

◆

Sandboxed shell

Crowe Talon runs tools inside an NVIDIA NemoClaw sandbox on a Brev VM. Shell commands can't touch your filesystem. One-click Brev Launchable included.

◆

Live cost visibility

Every turn shows tokens, cache hits, USD cost, credits consumed. You always know what a conversation is actually burning.

◆

/replay and /fork

Re-run any past turn on a different model. Fork a conversation to try a different approach without losing context. A/B models like flipping a switch.

◆

Seven-provider chain

Anthropic, Azure OpenAI, NVIDIA NIM, Ollama Cloud, IBM Watsonx, OpenRouter, Moonshot direct. Automatic fallback when one provider hits a ceiling.

FAQ

Honest answers to the obvious questions.

Why would I pay $29 when Claude Pro is $20? ›

Claude Pro gives you one model in one interface. Crowe Logic Foundry runs two flagship models concurrently and synthesizes their outputs, plus a sandboxed shell, plus a 98-tool registry spanning web search, browser automation, MCP, Arizona public records, and platform APIs. If you only need chat, Claude Pro is fine. If you're actually doing research, writing code, or running investigations, Foundry earns its premium.

What are credits and how do they work? ›

Credits decouple what you pay us from what we pay upstream providers. A fast-model turn costs 1 credit, balanced 2, flagship 5. Dual mode sums both sides. Synthesis adds 5. Personal's 750 credits support roughly 150 flagship turns or 300 dual-mode turns per month. You can see exact cost and credit consumption live in the HUD as you work.

Can I use my own API keys? ›

Yes. The BYOK tier is $19/mo and you bring keys for Anthropic, OpenAI, Azure OpenAI, Moonshot, OpenRouter, or NVIDIA NIM. Crowe Logic provides the CLI, dual mode, synthesis, tool ecosystem, HUD, and updates. You pay your providers directly. No credit meter.

What's the sandboxed shell actually for? ›

When an agent needs to run shell commands (install dependencies, test code, probe a remote server) there's an implicit trust decision. Default tools run on your laptop. Crowe Talon runs inside an NVIDIA NemoClaw sandbox on a Brev VM you provision. Commands execute there, not on your host. You get agent code execution without exposing your filesystem. One-click Brev Launchable included for setup.

Is my data trained on or sold? ›

No. Crowe Logic Foundry doesn't send your conversations to any training pipeline by default. Turns go from your CLI to the provider you picked (Anthropic, Azure, Moonshot, etc.) and back. Providers have their own data policies, which you can read on their sites. On Enterprise plans you get additional data retention controls and optional on-premises install.

Can I cancel anytime? ›

Yes. Monthly plans cancel at the end of your billing period. Annual plans are prepaid. No lock-in, no cancellation friction. Your conversation history remains in your local install regardless.

Who builds this? ›

Crowe Logic, Inc., founded by Michael Crowe. We ship the CLI as our primary product. The same framework powers other Crowe Logic AI agents (cultivation advisor, research, compliance) sold to adjacent markets. We're a bootstrapped company, not VC-backed. The product exists because we needed it for our own work.
